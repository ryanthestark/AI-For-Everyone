### Week 1-2: What is AI?

**Learning Goal**: Kids understand what AI is in simple terms.

#### Lesson: What is AI? (30-45 min)

**Parent Introduction:**
"AI stands for Artificial Intelligence. It's like a very smart computer program that can help us with questions, stories, and ideas. It's kind of like asking a robot friend for help!"

**Activity 1: AI vs Not-AI Game (15 min)**

Show kids examples and ask: "Is this AI?"

‚úÖ **IS AI:**
- Siri or Alexa answering questions
- YouTube recommending videos you might like
- Auto-correct on your phone
- ChatGPT helping with homework
- Netflix suggesting shows

‚ùå **NOT AI:**
- A calculator (it just does math)
- A book
- A video game character following a set path
- A timer or alarm clock

**Discussion**: "AI learns from lots of information and makes decisions. A calculator just follows one rule!"

**Activity 2: First AI Conversation (15 min)**

**Parent & child together:**
1. Open ChatGPT or Google Gemini
2. Type together: "Hello! I'm [child's first name] and I'm learning about AI. Can you explain what AI is for a [age]-year-old?"
3. Read response together
4. Ask follow-up: "Can you give me an example?"

**Reflection Questions:**
- "What did the AI tell you?"
- "Was it helpful?"
- "What else do you want to know?"

**Homework (Optional):**
- Draw a picture of what you think AI looks like
- Tell a family member one thing you learned

### Week 3-4: Prompts vs Questions

**Learning Goal**: Kids learn how to ask AI good questions (prompts).

#### Lesson: How to Talk to AI (30-45 min)

**Parent Introduction:**
"When we talk to AI, we call it a 'prompt.' A good prompt is like giving clear instructions. Let's practice!"

**Activity 1: Bad Prompt vs Good Prompt (20 min)**

**Try this with your child:**

**Bad Prompt**: "Tell me about dogs"
- What happens: AI gives a VERY long, boring answer

**Good Prompt**: "Tell me 3 fun facts about dogs for a [age]-year-old"
- What happens: Short, interesting answer!

**Bad Prompt**: "Story"
- What happens: AI confused or gives random story

**Good Prompt**: "Write a short story about a brave cat who saves the day"
- What happens: Fun, specific story!

**Activity 2: Prompt Practice (20 min)**

**Have kids practice making prompts better:**

| Bad Prompt | Help them make it better! |
|------------|---------------------------|
| "Tell me about space" | "Tell me 5 cool facts about planets for kids" |
| "Help with math" | "Explain how to add fractions like I'm 8 years old" |
| "Write something" | "Write a funny poem about my dog" |

**The Magic Words:**
- "For a [age]-year-old"
- "In simple words"
- "Give me 3 examples"
- "Make it short and fun"

**Activity 3: Challenge Round (10 min)**

"Ask AI to help you with something YOU want to know!"

Examples:
- "Why is the sky blue? Explain for a 7-year-old."
- "What are some fun facts about penguins?"
- "Can you help me understand why we have seasons?"

**Reflection:**
- "Which prompt worked better?"
- "What helped you get a good answer?"

### Week 5-6: Making AI Your Reading Buddy

**Learning Goal**: Kids use AI to help with reading and understanding.

#### Lesson: AI Can Help You Read! (30-45 min)

**Parent Introduction:**
"Sometimes books or homework use hard words. AI can be like a reading helper!"

**Activity 1: Vocabulary Helper (15 min)**

**Pick a book your child is reading. Find a tricky word.**

**Try these prompts:**
1. "What does the word [tricky word] mean for a [age]-year-old?"
2. "Can you use the word [tricky word] in a sentence?"
3. "What's a simpler word I could use instead of [tricky word]?"

**Example:**
- Word: "Determined"
- Prompt: "What does 'determined' mean for an 8-year-old? Give me an example."
- AI Response: "'Determined' means you really want to do something and you don't give up! Like if you're determined to learn to ride a bike, you keep trying even if you fall."

**Activity 2: Story Summaries (15 min)**

**After reading a chapter:**

Prompt: "I just read a chapter about [brief description]. Can you ask me 3 questions to see if I understood it?"

**AI will ask questions!** Kid answers them. Parent checks.

**Activity 3: Reading Comprehension (15 min)**

**Use AI for book discussions:**

Prompts to try:
- "I'm reading [book name]. What do you think will happen next?"
- "What's the main lesson in [book name]?"
- "Who is your favorite character in [book name] and why?"

**Note to Parents**: This is GREAT for homeschool reading discussions!

**Reflection:**
- "How did AI help you understand your book better?"
- "What was your favorite way to use AI for reading?"

### Week 1-2: AI as Your Art Partner

**Learning Goal**: Kids use AI to brainstorm and describe art projects.

#### Lesson: Creating Art Ideas with AI (45-60 min)

**Parent Introduction:**
"AI can't draw for you yet (in this curriculum), but it can help you get amazing ideas!"

**Activity 1: Drawing Prompt Generator (20 min)**

**Ask AI for drawing ideas:**

Prompts:
- "Give me 5 fun drawing ideas for a [age]-year-old"
- "I want to draw an animal. Give me a creative animal idea!"
- "Create a character idea for a story: describe what they look like"

**Then**: Kid draws one of the ideas!

**Activity 2: Color and Style Helper (15 min)**

**Teaching color combinations:**

Prompts:
- "What colors go well together for a sunset drawing?"
- "I'm drawing a forest. What colors should I use?"
- "Suggest a color palette for a underwater scene"

**Activity 3: Art Project Planner (20 min)**

**Use AI to plan a craft:**

Prompt: "I want to make a craft project about [topic]. I'm [age] years old. Give me step-by-step instructions using materials I have at home like paper, glue, and markers."

**Example Projects**:
- Make a paper crown
- Create a family crest
- Design a superhero logo
- Build a paper airplane with decorations

**Reflection:**
- "What art idea was your favorite?"
- "How did AI help you be more creative?"

### Week 3-4: AI as Your Science Explainer

**Learning Goal**: Kids use AI to understand science concepts.

#### Lesson: Exploring Science with AI (45-60 min)

**Parent Introduction:**
"Science can be tricky, but AI can explain things in ways that make sense to YOU!"

**Activity 1: Science Question Time (20 min)**

**Ask "Why?" questions:**

Prompts for kids:
- "Why do we have day and night? Explain like I'm [age]."
- "How do plants grow? Make it simple and fun."
- "Why do some things float and others sink?"
- "What makes a rainbow? Explain for a kid."

**Activity 2: Science Experiments (25 min)**

**Use AI to plan an experiment:**

Prompt: "Give me a safe, simple science experiment I can do at home with [parent's name] help. I'm [age] years old. I want to learn about [topic like 'mixing colors' or 'magnets']."

**AI will give you:**
- Materials needed
- Steps to follow
- What to observe
- Why it works

**Then**: Do the experiment together!

**Activity 3: Curiosity Questions (15 min)**

**Kid asks their OWN science questions:**

Examples from kids:
- "Why is space dark?"
- "How do birds fly?"
- "What happens to water when it freezes?"
- "Why do I need to sleep?"

**Teaching Moment**: "AI is great for first answers, but always double-check science facts with books or parents!"

**Reflection:**
- "What science thing did you learn?"
- "What experiment do you want to try next?"

### Week 5-6: Multi-Step Thinking

**Learning Goal**: Kids learn to break big questions into smaller steps.

#### Lesson: Thinking in Steps (45-60 min)

**Parent Introduction:**
"Sometimes one question isn't enough. We can ask AI to help us step-by-step!"

**Activity 1: The Step-by-Step Game (20 min)**

**Big Question**: "How do I make a birthday card?"

**Bad Way**: Ask once, get overwhelming answer

**Good Way**: Ask in steps!
1. "What supplies do I need to make a birthday card?"
2. "How do I fold the paper for a card?"
3. "What should I write inside a birthday card for my friend?"
4. "Give me 3 decoration ideas for a birthday card"

**Practice**: Choose a project and break it into 4 steps with AI help.

**Activity 2: Recipe Builder (20 min)**

**Make a pretend recipe with AI:**

Step 1: "I want to create a recipe for [silly food like 'cloud cookies']. What ingredients would I need?"

Step 2: "How would I mix these ingredients?"

Step 3: "What should I do next?"

Step 4: "How do I know when it's done?"

**Then**: Draw pictures of your silly recipe!

**Activity 3: Planning a Project (20 min)**

**Use multi-step thinking for a real project:**

Example: "I want to build a cardboard fort"

Steps to ask AI:
1. "What materials do I need for a cardboard fort?"
2. "How do I connect cardboard pieces?"
3. "How can I make my fort strong?"
4. "What decorations can I add?"

**Reflection:**
- "How did breaking it into steps help?"
- "When else could you use step-by-step thinking?"

### Week 7-8: Fixing AI Mistakes

**Learning Goal**: Kids learn AI isn't perfect and how to check facts.

#### Lesson: AI Can Be Wrong! (45-60 min)

**Parent Introduction:**
"AI is super smart, but it makes mistakes! Let's learn how to double-check."

**Activity 1: Spot the Mistake (20 min)**

**Try prompts that might give wrong answers:**

1. "What color is the sun?" (AI might say yellow, but it's actually white!)
2. "How many states are in the USA?" (AI should say 50)
3. "Can penguins fly?" (No!)

**Parent Task**: Intentionally ask AI something you know the answer to. Check if AI is correct!

**Discussion**: "How can we check if AI is right?"

Checking Methods:
- Ask a parent or teacher
- Look in a book
- Search on Google (with parent)
- Ask AI to explain more

**Activity 2: Fact-Checking Practice (20 min)**

**AI Response Detective:**

1. Ask AI: "Tell me 3 facts about [animal/topic]"
2. Pick one fact
3. Look it up together in a book or search
4. Was AI right?

**Activity 3: Fixing Bad Answers (15 min)**

**If AI gives a confusing answer, try:**

- "Can you explain that more simply?"
- "I didn't understand. Can you explain like I'm [younger age]?"
- "Can you give me an example?"
- "That doesn't sound right. Can you check?"

**Reflection:**
- "What mistake did AI make?"
- "How did you figure out it was wrong?"
- "What will you do next time AI's answer seems weird?"

### Week 7-8: Introduction to Coding (Scratch with AI Help)

**Learning Goal**: Kids start learning basic coding concepts with AI as a helper.

#### Lesson 1: What is Coding? (Week 7)

**Activity: Coding Concepts with AI (60 min)**

**Introduction Prompts:**
- "Explain what coding is for a [age]-year-old who has never coded before"
- "What is Scratch? How does it help kids learn to code?"

**Activity**: Watch Scratch tutorial together, then ask AI:
- "I'm learning Scratch. How do I make a sprite move?"

#### Lesson 2: AI-Assisted Scratch Project (Week 8)

**Activity: Build a Game with AI Help (60-90 min)**

**Project**: Make a simple catching game

**Prompts to use:**
1. "I'm making a Scratch game where a character catches falling objects. What blocks do I need?"
2. "How do I make objects fall from the top of the screen in Scratch?"
3. "How do I keep score in Scratch?"
4. "I'm stuck on [specific problem]. Can you help?"

**Result**: Kid builds their first game with AI as a coding assistant!

**Reflection**:
- "What did you create?"
- "How did AI help when you got stuck?"

### Week 9-10: Kid Research Projects

**Learning Goal**: Kids conduct independent research projects with AI assistance.

#### Lesson: Full Research Project (2 weeks, 90-120 min total)

**Step 1: Choose a Topic (20 min)**

Kid chooses something they're curious about:
- Animals (sharks, pandas, butterflies)
- Countries (Japan, Egypt, Brazil)
- Historical figures (Marie Curie, MLK Jr.)
- Science topics (volcanoes, space, weather)
- Hobbies (soccer, art, music)

**Step 2: Research with AI (40 min)**

**Research Prompts:**
- "Give me 10 interesting facts about [topic] for a kid"
- "What are the most important things to know about [topic]?"
- "Tell me about the history of [topic]"
- "What are some fun facts most people don't know about [topic]?"

**Important**: Cross-check facts with books or websites!

**Step 3: Organize Information (20 min)**

**Prompt AI to help organize:**
- "Help me organize these facts into categories"
- "What would be a good outline for a presentation about [topic]?"

**Step 4: Create Final Project (30 min)**

**Options:**
- Poster with drawings and facts
- PowerPoint/Google Slides presentation
- Written report
- Video script
- Comic book about the topic

**Use AI to help:**
- "Give me ideas for how to present [topic] to my family"
- "Help me write an introduction paragraph about [topic]"
- "What images or diagrams would help explain [topic]?"

**Step 5: Present to Family! (20 min)**

Kid presents their project to family. Be their first audience!

**Reflection:**
- "What was the coolest thing you learned?"
- "How did AI help you with your project?"
- "What would you research next?"

## üåü Tips for Parents

### If You Know Nothing About AI:

**You don't need to be an expert!** This curriculum teaches you AND your kid together.

**Tips:**
- Learn alongside your child
- It's okay to say "I don't know, let's find out together!"
- Read each lesson ahead of time
- Try the activities yourself first if nervous
- Join online parent groups for support

### For Homeschool Families:

**This curriculum integrates easily:**
- Use AI for reading comprehension (Language Arts)
- Use AI for math worksheet generation (Math)
- Use AI for science explanations (Science)
- Use AI research for history/social studies
- Use AI art prompts for art class

**Scheduling**:
- 3x per week, 30-60 min sessions = 5-6 months
- 2x per week, 60 min sessions = 6-8 months
- 1x per week, 90 min sessions = 8-10 months

### For Neurodivergent Learners:

**AI can be especially helpful for:**
- Kids with dyslexia (AI as reading helper)
- ADHD learners (short, focused prompts)
- Autistic kids (clear, structured responses)
- Kids with writing challenges (AI helps organize thoughts)

**Adaptations:**
- Break lessons into smaller chunks
- Use more visual examples
- Allow extra time
- Repeat activities if needed
- Let kid lead with their interests

### Multi-Age Teaching:

**If you have kids of different ages:**
- Pair older kid (10-12) with younger (7-9) for buddy learning
- Older kid helps younger with prompts
- Each does age-appropriate activity from same lesson
- Teach collaboration and patience

## ‚ùì FAQ for Parents

**Q: My child is bored with the curriculum. What do I do?**
A: Skip ahead! Or let them lead with their own project ideas. Interest-driven learning is powerful.

**Q: My child is struggling. Should we stop?**
A: Go slower! Repeat activities. Break lessons into smaller pieces. Make it fun, not frustrating.

**Q: How much should I help vs let them explore?**
A: Ages 7-8: High involvement. Ages 9-10: Moderate guidance. Ages 11-12: Nearby but independent.

**Q: Is this enough to make my kid an "AI expert"?**
A: This is a foundation. Think of it like learning to read‚Äîit opens doors to everything else!

**Q: Can I use this curriculum with a group/class?**
A: Yes! Works great for homeschool co-ops, after-school programs, or library groups.
