## üéØ Lab Goal

Learn how to use free AI tools (ChatGPT or Claude) to create a personalized, effective study plan for any subject you're learning.

* **Level:** Beginner

## üìã Prerequisites

**Required:**
* Basic ability to use a web browser
* A subject or topic you want to study (can be anything!)

**Helpful but not required:**
* Understanding of your own learning style
* Knowledge of how much time you have to study

## üìñ What You'll Learn

By completing this lab, you will:

* Understand how to craft effective prompts for AI assistants
* Learn to create structured, personalized study plans using AI
* Practice iterating and refining AI outputs to meet your needs
* Develop critical thinking skills about AI-generated content
* Gain confidence using AI as a learning partner

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Notes

**Before you begin:**

* **Privacy:** Don't share sensitive personal information (grades, school name, full name, etc.) with AI tools
* **Verification:** AI can make mistakes or provide outdated information - always verify critical facts
* **Learning tool:** Use AI to help organize your learning, not replace actually studying
* **Academic honesty:** Check your school's policies on AI use for homework and assignments

**Remember:**
* AI-generated study plans are starting points, not perfect solutions
* You know yourself best - adapt the plan to fit your needs
* The AI doesn't know your specific curriculum or teacher's expectations
* Use multiple sources when studying important topics

## üöÄ Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Prepare Your Information (Time: 3-5 minutes)

**Objective:** Gather the information you need to create an effective study plan

**Instructions:**

1. **Choose your subject or topic**
   * Be specific: "Algebra 1" is better than "math"
   * Example: "Spanish vocabulary for travel," "Biology - Cell Structure," "Python programming basics"

2. **Determine your timeframe**
   * How long until your test, project, or goal?
   * How much time can you study each day/week?
   * Example: "2 weeks, 30 minutes per day"

3. **Identify your goals**
   * What do you want to achieve?
   * Example: "Pass the midterm exam," "Have basic conversation skills," "Build a simple program"

4. **Know your starting level**
   * Complete beginner, some experience, or reviewing?
   * Example: "I know basic vocab but struggle with verb conjugation"

**Example Setup:**
```
Subject: World History - World War II
Timeframe: 10 days, 45 minutes per day
Goal: Score 85% or higher on upcoming test
Starting level: Completed reading assignments but need to review and organize notes
```

**Tips:**
* üí° The more specific you are, the better the AI can help
* ‚ö†Ô∏è Don't share personal details like your school name or teacher's name

**Checkpoint:** ‚úÖ You have a clear subject, timeframe, goal, and starting level written down

---

### Step 2: Create Your Initial Prompt (Time: 3-5 minutes)

**Objective:** Write an effective prompt that provides the AI with the context it needs

**Instructions:**

1. **Open your chosen AI tool**
   * Go to [chat.openai.com](https://chat.openai.com) or [claude.ai](https://claude.ai)
   * Sign in to your free account

2. **Write a detailed prompt using this structure:**

```
I need help creating a study plan. Here's my situation:

Subject: [Your specific subject/topic]
Timeframe: [How many days/weeks and time per day]
Goal: [What you want to achieve]
Current level: [Your starting point]
Learning style: [If you know it - visual, hands-on, reading, etc.]

Please create a day-by-day study plan that breaks down what I should focus on each session.
```

3. **Customize with additional details** (optional but helpful):
   * Specific topics you need to cover
   * Areas where you struggle
   * Resources you have (textbook, notes, videos)
   * Preferred study methods

**Example Prompt:**

```
I need help creating a study plan. Here's my situation:

Subject: Biology - Cell Structure and Function
Timeframe: 12 days, 40 minutes per day (Monday-Friday, 1 hour on weekends)
Goal: Understand all cell organelles, their functions, and how they work together
Current level: I've read the chapter once but I'm confused about the differences between plant and animal cells
Learning style: I learn best with diagrams and by making flashcards

Please create a day-by-day study plan that breaks down what I should focus on each session. Include specific topics for each day and suggest study activities.
```

**Tips:**
* üí° Be honest about your current level - the AI can't help if it doesn't know where you're starting
* üí° Mention any upcoming quizzes or tests so the plan can build toward them
* ‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid sharing your specific school, class schedule, or teacher names

**Checkpoint:** ‚úÖ You have a complete prompt ready to send to the AI

---

### Step 3: Submit Your Prompt and Review the Response (Time: 3-5 minutes)

**Objective:** Get your initial study plan and evaluate its quality

**Instructions:**

1. **Send your prompt to the AI**
   * Paste or type your prompt into the chat
   * Press Enter or click Send

2. **Wait for the complete response**
   * The AI will take 10-30 seconds to generate the plan
   * Let it finish completely before responding

3. **Review the study plan carefully**
   * Does it cover all the topics you need?
   * Is the daily breakdown realistic for your schedule?
   * Are the suggested activities helpful?
   * Does it match your learning style?

4. **Take notes on what to adjust**
   * Too much material per day?
   * Missing important topics?
   * Activities that won't work for you?
   * Need more detail or less detail?

**Example AI Response (abbreviated):**

```
Here's your 12-day Biology study plan:

Day 1 (40 min): Introduction to Cell Theory
- Read textbook section on cell discovery
- Watch 10-minute video on cell theory basics
- Create a simple timeline of cell biology discoveries

Day 2 (40 min): Cell Membrane & Transport
- Review cell membrane structure
- Learn active vs passive transport
- Make flashcards for key terms: osmosis, diffusion, etc.

[... continues with Days 3-12 ...]

Day 12 (60 min): Review & Practice Test
- Quick review of all flashcards
- Take practice quiz on all topics
- Focus extra time on weak areas
```

**Tips:**
* üí° The first version is rarely perfect - that's normal!
* üí° Look for a good balance between review and new learning
* ‚ö†Ô∏è If the plan seems too advanced or too basic, you'll need to adjust in the next step

**Checkpoint:** ‚úÖ You've received a complete study plan and identified what needs adjustment

---

### Step 4: Refine Your Study Plan (Time: 5-10 minutes)

**Objective:** Iterate with the AI to improve the plan to perfectly fit your needs

**Instructions:**

1. **Ask for specific adjustments**
   * Use follow-up prompts in the same conversation
   * Be specific about what to change

2. **Example refinement prompts:**

```
"This looks good, but Day 3 has too much material. Can you break it into 2 days?"
```

```
"I don't have access to videos. Can you replace video activities with reading and diagram-drawing instead?"
```

```
"Can you add more practice problems on Days 7-9? I need more hands-on practice."
```

```
"This is great! Can you also create a list of key terms I should know for each topic?"
```

3. **Continue refining until satisfied**
   * You can ask multiple follow-up questions
   * Each response builds on the previous conversation
   * The AI remembers the context

4. **Common adjustments to request:**
   * Adjust time per day
   * Add or remove topics
   * Change activity types
   * Add practice questions
   * Include review sessions
   * Create study resources (flashcard lists, practice questions)

**Example Refinement Conversation:**

```
You: "This is helpful! But I actually have a quiz on Day 6 covering Days 1-5 material. 
Can you adjust the plan so Days 4-5 focus on reviewing the early topics?"

AI: "Absolutely! Here's the revised plan with Days 4-5 focused on review before your quiz..."

You: "Perfect! Can you also create a 20-question practice quiz covering Days 1-5 
material that I can use to prepare?"

AI: "Of course! Here's a 20-question practice quiz..."
```

**Tips:**
* üí° Don't be afraid to ask for changes - the AI is there to help you
* üí° You can ask for study resources like flashcards, quiz questions, or summaries
* üí° Request different formats: checklists, calendars, or simple lists
* ‚ö†Ô∏è Remember the AI doesn't know your specific textbook content - use it for structure and organization

**Checkpoint:** ‚úÖ You have a study plan that fits your schedule, learning style, and goals

---

### Step 5: Save and Adapt Your Plan (Time: 3-5 minutes)

**Objective:** Capture your plan in a format you can actually use

**Instructions:**

1. **Copy your final study plan**
   * Select the text and copy it
   * Or use the copy button if available in the AI interface

2. **Save it in a useful format:**
   * Paste into a notes app (Google Docs, Notion, Apple Notes)
   * Create a physical checklist you can mark off
   * Add to your calendar or planner
   * Print it out to keep at your desk

3. **Make it actionable:**
   * Break down each day into specific tasks
   * Add checkboxes for completed items
   * Include any resources you need (textbook pages, links)

4. **Set up tracking:**
   * Create a simple way to mark progress
   * Note which days you complete
   * Track areas that need more time

**Example Format in Your Notes:**

```
Biology Study Plan - Cell Structure (12 Days)

Week 1:
‚òê Day 1 (Mon): Cell Theory basics - Read Chapter 1, watch intro video
‚òê Day 2 (Tue): Cell Membrane - Make flashcards for terms
‚òê Day 3 (Wed): Organelles Part 1 - Draw and label diagrams
‚òê Day 4 (Thu): Review Days 1-3 - Practice quiz
‚òê Day 5 (Fri): Final review before quiz

[... continue ...]

Notes:
- Struggled with: [fill in as you go]
- Need to review more: [fill in as you go]
```

**Tips:**
* üí° Add your own notes and insights as you follow the plan
* üí° Build in flexibility - life happens, and you may need to adjust
* üí° Keep the conversation with the AI open - you can come back to ask more questions
* ‚ö†Ô∏è Don't just save it and forget it - put it somewhere you'll actually see and use it

**Checkpoint:** ‚úÖ Your study plan is saved in a format you can easily follow and track

---

### Step 6: Execute and Adjust (Time: Ongoing)

**Objective:** Put your plan into action and refine as you learn

**Instructions:**

1. **Start following your plan**
   * Begin with Day 1 as outlined
   * Check off completed tasks
   * Stay consistent

2. **Monitor your progress:**
   * Note which topics are easier/harder than expected
   * Track how long activities actually take
   * Identify gaps in your understanding

3. **Make adjustments as needed:**
   * Go back to the AI if you need to modify the plan
   * Example: "I finished Day 3 material in 25 minutes instead of 40. Can you suggest additional practice for the extra time?"
   * Example: "Day 5's material was harder than expected. Can you break it into 2 days?"

4. **Use the AI for ongoing support:**
   * Ask for explanations of confusing concepts
   * Request practice problems
   * Get quiz question suggestions
   * Find additional resources

**Example Ongoing Prompts:**

```
"I'm on Day 4 of my study plan and I'm struggling with understanding active transport. 
Can you explain it in a simpler way with a real-world analogy?"
```

```
"I finished Days 1-6 faster than planned. Can you suggest advanced practice activities 
for the extra time I have?"
```

**Tips:**
* üí° Your study plan is a guide, not a rigid rule - adapt as you learn
* üí° Come back to the AI whenever you hit a roadblock
* üí° Celebrate small wins - completing each day is progress!
* ‚ö†Ô∏è Remember to actually study the material, not just follow the plan mechanically

**Checkpoint:** ‚úÖ You're actively using your plan and adjusting as needed

## üîç Troubleshooting

### Issue: AI's Plan is Too Generic or Vague

**Symptoms:**
* Plan says things like "study chapter" without specific activities
* No clear learning objectives for each day
* Doesn't match your specific needs

**Solutions:**
1. Provide more specific information in your initial prompt
2. Ask follow-up: "Can you be more specific about what activities I should do on Day 3?"
3. Request: "Break down each day's tasks into 5-10 minute chunks"
4. Share more context about your learning goals and current challenges

---

### Issue: Too Much Material Per Day

**Symptoms:**
* You can't complete a day's work in the time allotted
* Feeling overwhelmed by the amount to cover

**Solutions:**
1. Ask: "This seems like too much for [X] minutes. Can you reduce the scope?"
2. Request: "Spread this same content over [X] more days"
3. Prioritize: "What are the most important topics I should focus on if I have less time?"

---

### Issue: Plan Doesn't Match Your Resources

**Symptoms:**
* Suggests videos you can't access
* Recommends tools or materials you don't have
* Activities that don't fit your situation

**Solutions:**
1. Specify your available resources: "I only have a textbook and my class notes, no online videos"
2. Ask for substitutions: "Replace all video suggestions with reading-based activities"
3. Request alternatives: "Suggest activities I can do with just paper and pencil"

---

### Issue: Not Sure How to Start

**Symptoms:**
* Feeling overwhelmed by the whole process
* Unsure what information to provide

**Solutions:**
1. Start with a simple prompt: "I need to study [subject] for a test in [timeframe]. Where should I start?"
2. Let the AI ask you questions to gather information
3. Try this starter: "Help me create a study plan. What information do you need from me?"
